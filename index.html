Lucas Chen

For a while, I walked through the fields and towns of Man. I was neither borne in their festivities nor caught in their strife. Not able to stay in any one place, I had no blessing of a destination either. Though the weariness in my sole reminded me that such travels could not continue forever, I couldn't accept returning to the flatside with its endless waves of oblivion and eternal dead sun. I feared that from the core of my existence. So I wandered on, through the night and the day, through the seasons, through the years.
